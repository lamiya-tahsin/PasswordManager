Password Tips

Use a combination of character types:

  • Uppercase letters: A-Z
  • Lowercase letters: a-z
  • Numbers: 0-9
  • Symbols: ~`!@#$%^&*()_-+={[}]|:;"'<,>.?/

Avoid common patterns and easily guessable passwords:

  • Do not use dictionary words or permutations of such.
  • Avoid using common words, names, technical jargon, and keyboard sequences.
  • Do not base your password on personal information like your name, birthday, or social security number.

Create strong, memorable passwords:

  • Use random, pronounceable syllables to form words that are easy to remember.
  • Create acronyms for unique phrases and substitute characters for complexity.
  • Combine two separate words and apply character substitution for added complexity.

Utilize character substitution:

  • Replace certain letters with symbols, numbers, or uppercase letters to enhance complexity.
  • Combine words or phrases creatively:
  • Insert numbers between letters or use patterns for increased complexity.

Phrase-based passwords:

  • Use statements, locations, or lines from books/movies and apply character substitution.
